For adult patients with symptomatic anaemia of CKD, EVRENZO is like a breath of fresh air, at high altitude

Anaemia is a common complication of chronic kidney disease3 which can leave patients feeling tired, weak and breathless.4

But the discovery of the HIF (hypoxia-inducible factor) pathway, which stimulates erythropoiesis when oxygen levels fall, has opened up a new approach to treating anaemia of CKD.1,5

EVRENZO (roxadustat) is a first-in-class HIF-PH inhibitor5 which mimics the body’s natural response to hypoxia.1

By stimulating a coordinated erythropoietic response,1,2 EVRENZO helps adult patients with symptomatic anaemia of CKD to reach and maintain their target Hb levels.2

The Phase III DOLOMITES study evaluated the efficacy and safety of EVRENZO compared to darbepoetin-alfa for the treatment of anaemia in patients with NND stage 3-5 CKD. **Target range was 10.0-12.0 g/dL.6

The primary endpoint analysis demonstrated non-inferiority of EVRENZO to darbepoetin-alfa in the proportion of patients achieving Hb response during the first 24 weeks. 89.5% of EVRENZO-treated patients achieved a Hb response vs. 78.0% of patients receiving darbepoetin-alfa during the first 24 weeks of treatment.6

During clinical development, the general safety profile of EVRENZO was overall comparable to ESAs for the majority of safety variables evaluated and is reflective of the studied CKD populations.6,7-9 Please see EVRENZO SmPC for the full safety profile.

EVRENZO provides:

  • Proven Hb efficacy,6 with a reduced use of IV iron compared with ESA6
  • No increase in risk of cardiovascular events compared with ESA in patients in NDD-CKD and IDD-CKD†*1
  • The convenience of an oral tablet taken three times per week on non-consecutive days1
